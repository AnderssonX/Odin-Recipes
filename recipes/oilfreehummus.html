Oil free hummus

Back

Description

A spicy soup that will warm your soul and comfort your heart.

Ingredients

  • canned chickpeas
  • baking soda
  • aquafaba
  • lemon juice
  • garlic
  • tahini: aka ground sesame seeds. You can try substituting almond butter or peanut butter if you don't have any tahini.
  • ground cumin
  • sea salt
  • ice-cold water

Steps

  1. Place a mesh strainer over a bowl and drain the canned chickpeas, catching the aquafaba. Set the aquafaba aside. (If you forget to do this it's not the end of the world. You can still make this recipe, you will probably just need to add a bit more water.)

  2. Add the chickpeas and baking soda to a medium pot and fill halfway with water. Bring to a boil, then turn down the heat to medium-high and cook for about 15 minutes or until the chickpeas are mushy and soft.

  3. While the chickpeas are boiling, grate the garlic clove and add it to a small bowl with the lemon juice. This will help mellow out the garlic flavor. Set aside.

  4. When the chickpeas are done, drain and rinse to remove the excess baking soda.

  5. Add the chickpeas, lemon garlic mixture, aquafaba, tahini, cumin, and salt to a food processor and blend until combined.

  6. To get the hummus extra creamy, blend again on high and add 1 to 2 tablespoons of ice-cold water to the top of the food processor while it's running.

  7. When you've achieved your desired creaminess level, taste the hummus to adjust seasonings if necessary.

  8. Garnish with extra aquafaba and paprika and serve right away or store for later.
